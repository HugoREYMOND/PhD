Alumina powder is used to cast the customized melt nozzle and the inner layer of the crucible. Indeed, the crucible is made of graphite and shouldn't be in contact with the melt. The alumina powder is milled to obtained very thin particles. By mixing it with the right amount of water, this results to a non-Newtonian paste which will be poured in the proper mold. The mold is usually 3D printed with PLA or PETG by fusion deposition modeling. A layer of Vaseline is applied before the cast to make the demolding process easier. In order to minimize the cracks when drying, the amount of water has to be minimized as much as possible, which also make the pouring more difficult. With a mechanical vibrating support beneath the mold, the paste can be made even more viscous, to the point where it would not be able to flow into the mold without the assistance of vibration. Indeed, the strange properties of the obtained paste make it much more fluid when it is vibrated, and come back to solid past when the vibration is stopped.\\

For the crucible inner layer, the about 2~kg of alumina powder is used, with about 800~ml of water.
